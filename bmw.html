BMW

The BMW Group is a German multinational manufacturer of luxury vehicles and motorcycles headquartered in Munich, Bavaria, Germany. The company was founded in 1916 as a manufacturer of aircraft engines, which it produced from 1917 to 1918 and again from 1933 to 1945 creating engines for aircraft that were used in the Second World War. The companies automobiles are marketed under the BMW, Mini and Rolls-Royce brands, and motorcycles are marketed under the BMW Motorrad brand. Also in 2023, BMW was the world's ninth-largest producer of motor vehicles, with 2,555,341 vehicles produced and in 2023 the 6th largest by revenue.In 2023, the company was ranked 46th in the Forbes Global 2000.The company has significant motor-sport history, especially in touring cars, sports cars, and the Isle of Man TT.

History

BMW is founded in Munich, Germany, focusing on aircraft engine production.The company produces its first motorcycle, the BMW R32, which features the iconic flat-twin engine layout. BMW enters the automobile market by acquiring Automobilwerk Eisenach, producing the BMW 3/15.The BMW 328 sports car gains fame for its performance and wins numerous races.During WWII, BMW shifts back to military production, focusing on aircraft engines and motorcycles.BMW's facilities are damaged, and the company faces significant challenges.
BMW returns to motorcycle production, launching models like the R51/3.Facing financial difficulties, BMW is saved by a deal with the German government and begins to focus on automobiles again.The BMW 1500 is introduced, marking the beginning of the "New Class" series, which revitalizes the brand.
The BMW 5 Series debuts, establishing the brand in the luxury segment. The 3 Series is launched, becoming one of the best-selling models in automotive history.
BMW acquires the British brand Rover, though this partnership proves less successful, leading to its eventual sale in 2000. The BMW X5 is introduced, marking BMW's entry into the SUV market.

BMW continues to innovate in electric mobility and autonomous driving, maintaining its reputation for performance and luxury.

Slogan

The slogan 'The Ultimate Driving Machine' was first used in North America in 1974.In 2010, this long-lived campaign was mostly supplanted by a campaign intended to make the brand more approachable and to better appeal to women, 'Joy'. By 2012 BMW had returned to 'The Ultimate Driving Machine'.
